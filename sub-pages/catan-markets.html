Markets of Catan — Rules

Markets of Catan

A variant of Settlers of Catan introducing money, a fluctuating resource market, and new ways to build, trade, and raid.

Setup

Standard 6-player Catan setup, with the following changes:

  • Each player starts with 2 gold.
  • All 3:1 ports are replaced with random 2:1 ports.
  • Additional components required:
    • A bowl of coins (gold)
    • The market prices board with markers (or a digital tracker)
    • Storehouse tokens
    • Market stall tokens

Money

Money is the universal medium of exchange in Catan's prosperous markets. Rather than hauling lumber through the streets looking for a trade, settlers now carry coin purses.

  • You may sell any resource card for 1 gold at any time, even when it is not your turn.
  • You may buy resources at the current market price only on your turn.
  • There is no limit to how much gold you can hold.
  • You may trade resources, gold, or both with other players. Any trade must involve the active player.
  • You cannot trade with the bank under any circumstances, even if you have a port.

Ports

Ports allow you to export the listed resource for a better rate.

  • A 2:1 port lets you sell the listed resource for 3 gold instead of 1.
  • Ports do not affect buying; you still buy at market price.

Hand Limit

All resource cards are kept face up. Development cards remain face down.

  • Starting hand limit is 6 resource cards.
  • If you would exceed your limit at any time (on or off your turn), you must immediately sell or use resources to get back within the limit.
  • Only resource cards count toward the hand limit. Gold and development cards do not.

Storehouses

Storehouses permanently increase your hand limit by 1 card each.

  • Prices escalate: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24 gold for your 1st, 2nd, 3rd, etc.
  • Storehouses cannot be lost or stolen.
  • You may purchase storehouses only on your turn.

Market Stalls

A market stall lets you sell any resource for 2 gold instead of 1.

  • Cost: 15 gold (one-time purchase).
  • Stacks with ports: if you have a port for a specific resource, the port rate (3 gold) still applies for that resource; the market stall rate applies to all other resources.
  • You may purchase a market stall only on your turn.

Building with Gold

In addition to the standard resource costs, you may build with gold:

  • Road: 4 gold
  • Settlement: 12 gold
  • City: 18 gold

You must pay either the full resource cost or the full gold cost — you cannot mix the two for a single purchase.

The Market

Resource prices fluctuate based on recent production across the island.

  • After each roll, record the resources produced by that roll. (Example: an 8 is rolled, and there are two hexes showing 8 — one wood, one ore. Record 1 wood and 1 ore.)
  • The market tracks the last 6 non-7 rolls. Rolls of 7 are not counted.
  • Prices update immediately after each roll.

Price Formula

For each resource, let N be the number of times it has been produced in the last 6 non-7 rolls.

Price = 6 − N, with a minimum of 2 gold.

Rolling a 7: The Hired Thief

Gold attracts thieves, and Catan's roads are not safe.

When a 7 is rolled, the roller chooses any player to steal gold from. The amount stolen is 2 × the target's victory points.

  • This steal cannot be blocked.
  • The roller may not target themselves.
  • The roller may not target the single player with the fewest victory points. If multiple players are tied for fewest, any of them may be targeted.
  • If the target has less gold than the amount owed, they lose all the gold they have. No difference is paid from the bank.

The traditional robber is not used; the thief deals only in gold and is not placed on the board.

Soldier Cards

Soldiers represent strong-arming a favorable deal in the market.

When you play a soldier on your turn, for that turn only:

  1. Sell phase: You may sell any number of resources from your hand at 3 gold each.
  2. Buy phase: You may then buy any number of resources at 1 gold each (up to your hand limit).

Sells and buys are resolved as two separate phases — you must complete all selling before any buying.

  • A played soldier is placed face-up in front of you and continues to count toward Largest Army.
  • The market discount/premium applies only on the turn the soldier is played.
  • Soldiers may only be played on your turn.

Turn Summary

On your turn:

  1. Roll dice. Distribute resources, or resolve a 7.
  2. Update the market tracker with the roll's produced resources (or skip if a 7).
  3. Trade, buy, sell, and build as desired. You may:
    • Buy resources at market price
    • Sell resources for 1 gold each (2 with a market stall, 3 with a port)
    • Trade with other players
    • Build roads, settlements, or cities (with resources or gold)
    • Buy storehouses or market stalls
    • Buy or play development cards
  4. End your turn.
